⋉ Adventures of Mouse-Cat ⋊

Part One: Rescue

We decided after some time of mourning the loss of our first cat, that home wasnt the same without a little furry friend to greet us everyday when we came home. So we drove up to the Humane Society to find us a baby kitten. We had two in mind, however when we got there, only one of them was still there. We were a little bummed as we wanted to make the perfect decision and one that bonded with us almost instantly and captured our hearts. Though we decided to at least wait for the one kitten, we figured never know, maybe this happened for a reason. We were waiting in the waiting area across the viewing rooms where you could view kittens and while waiting we noticed this older cat staring at us lovingly. We visited her momentarily and she was very loving from the start. She was 2 1/2 years old and was given up at 10 months by her first family and spent most of her life there, we knew we had to save her like she knew she had to save us as well, so we adopted our new furry child!

Part Two: Curiosity

When we first brought her home she was so curious about her surroundings. This was a whole new world for her and one she was no longer trapped in, she could finally explore! She is very smart, always coming when called and so adorable. She is very active in her exploring and wanting to get into everything and anything and She won our hearts instantly. At first we admit, it was still hard, we were very close to our other cat that we were not ready to let go of at her young age of five years old but she quickly helped us heal. We helped her heal as well as we realized also when we brought her home that she was so scared of any sudden movement or if she thought we would be mad about something. Though as time has gone on she too has been healing from that as well with us.

Part Three: Quirks

She has many odd traits for example we swear she thinks she is a monkey, a mouse and a meerkat all in one! Her tail is so cute because it curled like a monkey's tail and would hook onto things as well or wrap around your wrist. Whenever she meowed it isn't a cat's meow, it sounds more like a squeaking mouse! it is so adorable and makes her so unique! Whenever she hears noises she stands up on her two back legs like a meerkat does and she loves watching tv with us as well. She now knows she has a loving forever home and loving parents who will spoil her the rest of her life.
